Before You Start — Gmail App Password

Regular Gmail passwords will not work. You must generate an App Password.

▶
Watch: How to generate a Gmail App Password
youtu.be/ckfyTVgF1T4
1

Go to Google Account → Security and enable 2-Step Verification

2

Search "App Passwords" in your Google Account settings

3

Create a new App Password → copy the 16-character password and use it in the app

⚙️

Step 1 — Configure

Step 1 of 3
Field What to enter
Gmail Address Your Gmail (e.g. you@gmail.com)
App Password The 16-character password from above
Subject Your email subject line
Body Template Your message — use {{name}} where you want the recipient's name
Excel File Upload your recipients .xlsx file
PDF Attachment (optional) Any PDF up to 3 MB — attached to every email

Click Preview Recipients → when ready.

👁️

Step 2 — Preview

Step 2 of 3

Review the full list of recipients parsed from your Excel file. Confirm the attachment (if any), then click Send Emails to begin.

📤

Step 3 — Send

Step 3 of 3

Emails go out one by one with a 9-second gap between each. The dashboard shows live progress:

✓Total / Sent / Failed / Pending counters update in real time
✓Countdown timer shows seconds until the next email
✓Activity log records every success and failure
✓Hit Stop Sending anytime to cancel mid-campaign
📊

Excel File Format

recipients.xlsx
A B
1 Name Email
2 John Doe john.doe@example.com
3 Jane Smith jane.smith@example.com
4 Alice Johnson alice.j@example.com
✓First row must be the header: Name, Email
✓One recipient per row, saved as .xlsx
✓Column order doesn't matter — headers are detected automatically
✗Rows with missing or invalid emails are skipped automatically
✏️

Body Template Example

Hello {{name}},

Please find the attached document.

Regards,
Admin Team

{{name}} is automatically replaced with each recipient's name from the Excel file.
